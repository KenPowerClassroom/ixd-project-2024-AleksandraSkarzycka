Learning apps
Question Kahoot Typing.com CodeCombat
Engagement: How engaging is the app? Is it visually appealing? Does it have sound and animation to capture the user's attention? Does it have a good balance of challenge and reward? The app is pretty engaging, when finding a kahoot you want to play it's really easy with the search feature. During games there's animations that play when questions pop up, it also plays sounds that notify you if you've picked the right answer, as well as the wrong answer. It also awards you points for getting a question right, which then places you on the leaderboard against other players, making you want to play more to win against people. (8/10) Typing.com have starter videos that introduce you to what you're going to learn and how you're going to do it. The videos motivate the users to engage in the program to get better at typing. As you finish each level you are awarded stars for accuracy and you are also timed to see how fast you get through levels. Telling you your top WPM. (8/10) CodeCombat makes the experience pretty engaging as the user has to type in lines of code to move the character to reach a certain places, it lets you retry and gives you hints if you are stuck. There is music playing and the character walks after you run your code. (7/10)
Progress tracking: How well does the app track the user's progress? Does it provide feedback and recommendations to help users improve their performance? Does it use data to personalize the learning experience? Kahoot tracks the games you've played in the report section of your library. It tracks the % you got correct, as well as showing which questions you got correct or incorrect. You recieve feedback when requested at the end of each game you play. (10/10) It tells you your overall accuracy as you are completing a level, and the stars you earn in each section. It also tracks the amount of time you spend on each section that adds on to your overall time taken on that level. It keeps your average WPM speed but tells you how many WPM you get in each section of the game. In the end it shows how many stars you earned, and your average stats. (9/10) The app doesn't really track progress really well, it will only show you your score of the past level which you can't look in depth. The other way it tracks progress is by giving you achievements for completing certain levels but there is no in depth look at what you have done. (4/10)
User experience: How user-friendly is the app? Is it easy to navigate and use? Does it have clear instructions and prompts? Does it have a well-designed interface? Kahoot has a easy layout to get around on mobile. The home page shows differect sections like "Discover", "Partner Collections", "Categories" etc. which you can scroll through, or you could go to the Discover tab and search for specific kahoots you would want to play. (7/10) The layout of Typing.com is pretty simple. Everything is easy to find and access, you get a list of different typing games to try when you go to the website without needing to sign up to start the game. It is not complicated getting around at all. (10/10) The app is easy to navigate, it is easy to get around to different levels. It has clear instructions and prompts and has a well designed interface for a learning coding game. (7/10)
